My Life

Life in East Lansing

Joey Griffin
on 10/13/21

This school yeas has began with an incredible start, as many people have come back to live at MSU. East Lansing is bustling with life again and it is such a pleasure to see people walking around the city again. Throughout the day, buses and cars go by as people are on their way to class or work: and during the night, crowds walk through the city seeking some attraction. Often times, I see this as "Nature is Healing", people have finally returned to the college lifestyle and are enjoying every second of it. It's sweet, to see campus bustling with so many activities going on, and to see the active population taking part. With this positive start, I believe that the rest of this year will follow suit, with the spring semester finishing strong. Throughout this time, my schedule has become: wake up, go to class, come home, and then go out. It is truly an exhilirating experience, with so much to do, and tons and tons of new people to meet and hang out with. My only hope is that this kind of life can continue, even with some spikes in covid around the city and state. Nevertheless, I am enthralled to see East Lansing once alive again, returning to it's former pre-Covid glory.

2021 Summer Vacation

Joey Griffin
on 10/13/2021

This past summer was filled with oppurtunity and great times. For the first time in forever, I visited Florida again to see Universal Studios as well as DisneyWorld, and various other theme parks with my family. Shortly after, I made my long awaited trip back to California. I had lived there 3 years ago, and was desperate for a return to see my old friends. The first two days back in my home, I spent with some high school friends and explored Santa Cruz and the beach. After that, I went into San Francisco, with my father and two brothers: and we walked the Golden Gate Bridge, and traveled around all the different communities within the city. After the long day spent in SF, we went inland, towards the Bay Area, to visit our old city and schools. After waking up at our hotel, we drove around by some of our old schools, and visited our old house, even though I still call it my home. There was something special about visiting our old city, and seeing how most of it had remained unchained, as it was expecting our return. That my was favorite part of the trip, to come back to a place, and see it exactly as how I left it.
